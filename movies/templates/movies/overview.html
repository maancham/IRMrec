Study: A test collection for offline evaluation of recommender


Student Investigator: Houmaan Chamani
Faculty Supervisor: Prof. Mark D. Smucker, 519-888-4567 x38620
In this page, we explain what the study is about, possible risks and benefits, and your rights as a research participant. You may print/save a copy for your records. If you do not understand something in the letter, please ask one of the investigators before consenting to participate.

Project Summary:
The purpose of this study is to investigate the application of traditional information retrieval techniques to evaluate recommendation systems. The hypothesis is that by adapting established test collection construction methods from information retrieval, we can develop a robust evaluation framework for recommendation systems. The aim is to compare existing evaluation methods with a novel approach that incorporates user assessments of recommended movies.

Procedure:

  • There are two phases to this study. The first phase will take about 1.5 hours. After the first phase, if you’d like to continue to the second phase, and if your work meets a certain quality, you can participate in the second phase, which will take up to 5 hours.
  • If you consent to participate in this study, we will contact you with details on how you can export your ratings profile from Movielens, which will be used to generate personalized movie recommendations for you to judge in the two phases mentioned above. The ratings that you send us will become part of the study’s data.
  • It will take us approximately one week to process your ratings and produce recommendations. Once the movie recommendations are ready, we'll reach out to you and provide login credentials to the system. When you log in, you’ll first submit your demographics information. Next, you'll find a tutorial to complete on how to use the system. The judgment task is the core of this phase, and it involves reviewing a set of movies, around 100 in total. For each movie, we'll ask you three questions:
    • "How familiar are you with this movie?"
    • "How interested are you in watching this movie via a streaming service?"
    • "For unseen movies only: If you were to watch this movie, what would you predict your rating of it to be?"
  • Following these questions, you will be presented with the movies you are most interested in watching (based on your answers to the question above), and you will be asked to rank them (about 3 to 5 items). For the last part of the first phase, you will be asked to fill out a post-study questionnaire that aims to gather feedback on your overall experience.
  • As mentioned before, the first phase is expected to take up to 1.5 hours. At the end, we will ask you if you are interested in participating in the second phase of the study. If you are interested, we will update your profile and shortly contact you if you are selected to start the second phase. We will also be in contact to handle remuneration for the first phase.
  • The second phase is the same as the first one, except that there will be up to 1000 recommendations this time and it will take up to 5 hours. Upon completion, you can expect to see the top movie ranking and post-study questionnaire like before. We will handle the remuneration process from there.
  • To participate, you must be able to read, comprehend, and communicate in English. You must also have a laptop or desktop computer that you can use. A mobile device will not work. Your computer must be connected to the internet to be able to access our websites. Also, to participate in this study you must reside in USA or Canada and be over the age of 17.
  • We will collect your judgments of the recommendations as well as computer usage. You may stop participating in the study at any point and withdraw your consent without penalty.

Confidentiality and Data Security:
During the study, we will store all your responses to questions and all your interactions with the system. To keep your data unidentified, you will be issued an anonymous identifier in the form of a participant ID. During the study, you will use our website to answer questions and conduct web searches. We will not record your IP address. We will only use your anonymous ID to record your answers and behavior. All anonymized data collected will be retained for a minimum of 7 years and will only be used for research purposes. We may refer to individual participants when describing the results of the study, and in these cases, we will always refer to “Participant 1” or some other similar anonymous name. Your name will never appear in any publication that results from this study. Once you have finished participating in the study, we will destroy our record of the association of your name with your anonymous ID. If you want to withdraw your consent, you must do it before finishing participation. Once we have destroyed this association between your identity and the anonymous identifier, we will not be able to remove your data from our study.

Broad consent for the storage and future unspecified use of data:
We will make your Movielens ratings, collected computer behavioral data, decisions, and responses to all questions during the study, but excluding demographic data, available to other researchers who request access to it for similar research purposes as we have outlined, i.e., research on evaluation of recommendation systems. Researchers granted access to the data may be located outside of Canada and may not be subject to the same ethical standards (TCPS). While we will only release the data to other researchers, the use by these researchers will not be controlled by us. All data will be anonymized, but each participant’s data will remain identifiable as coming from an individual, i.e., “Participant 1”, “Participant 2”, etc. as such, the likelihood that someone could determine your identity from the data is very low. We will not publicly share this data, i.e., the data would only be made available to other researchers for research purposes. The data that we release to other researchers will be stored in an access-controlled repository (GitHub) and will not be openly available to the public. We and other researchers will not contact you regarding future research related to this data, for the data will be anonymized and not tied to your personal information.

Remuneration for Your Participation for phase one:
The duration of the first phase of this user study is 1.5 hours. You will be paid $30 CAD (if you reside in Canada) or $23 USD (if you reside in USA) as an Amazon.ca/Amazon.com e-gift card for the study. It is your responsibility to report this amount for income tax purposes.

Remuneration for Your Participation for phase two:
The duration of the second phase of this user study is estimated to be up to 5 hours. You will be paid an hourly rate of $20 CAD (if you reside in Canada) or $15 USD (if you reside in USA) as an Amazon.ca/Amazon.com e-gift card for the study. Should you stop before completing the study, you will be paid based on the amount of time spent doing judgement, calculated from website logs, rounded up to the nearest hour. It is your responsibility to report this amount for income tax purposes.

Risks and Benefits:
There is minimal risk to you from participation in this study. There are no direct benefits to you from participation. However, we hope the study will provide results that can lead to advances in the evaluation and development of recommender systems that will benefit society at large.

Research Ethics Clearance:
This study has been reviewed and received ethics clearance through a University of Waterloo Research Ethics Board (REB #45524) If you have questions for the Board, contact the Office of Research Ethics, toll-free at 1-833-643-2379 (Canada and USA), 1-519-888-4440, or reb@uwaterloo.ca. For all other questions regarding this project please contact the researchers listed above. Thank you for your interest in this project.
