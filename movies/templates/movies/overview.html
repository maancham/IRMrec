Evaluation of Recommendation Systems Research Study


Thank you for your interest in our research study. We are a research group at the University of Waterloo led by Prof. Mark Smucker. This study is part of the Master's thesis work of Houmaan Chamani and has been reviewed and received clearance through a University of Waterloo Research Ethics Board.

Goals of the Study:
In order to improve recommendation systems, we need to be able to accurately measure their quality. Our study aims to improve researchers' ability to measure recommendation system quality by collecting from participants, such as you, movie ratings and feedback beyond what you have already provided to Movielens. Our study works by first collecting from you your current Movielens ratings, and then we use your ratings to generate recommendations from a large number of different recommender algorithms. We then have you rate and give feedback on these recommendations so that we can measure the quality of these and future recommendation algorithms. The data we collect will form a long term resource available to researchers to advance the study of recommendation systems.

Summary of Participation Requirements and Remuneration:

  • To participate in the study, you must be 18 or older and a resident of the United States or Canada.
  • The study has two phases. The first phase will take you about 1.5 hours of time. The second phase is optional and may take up to an additional 5 hours of time.
  • To thank you for participating in the first phase, we will give you an Amazon e-gift card of $30 CAD or $23 US depending on whether you are a resident of the US or Canada.
  • If you participate in the second phase of the study, we will give you an Amazon e-gift card worth $15 US or $20 CAD per hour.
